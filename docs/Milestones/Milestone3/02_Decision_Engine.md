# Part A – Decision Engine Foundation

---

# 1. Executive Summary

The Decision Engine is the reasoning core of CityOps AI.

While the AI Infrastructure (Phase 1) provides communication with Gemini and prompt management, the Decision Engine determines **how the system thinks**.

Its responsibility is **not** to understand images directly or execute external tools.

Instead, it receives structured perception results, evaluates whether sufficient evidence exists, plans evidence collection when necessary, maintains reasoning state, and produces a structured operational decision.

The Decision Engine is intentionally deterministic.

It orchestrates reasoning rather than replacing it with unconstrained LLM behavior.

Every reasoning step must be explainable, bounded, and reproducible.

---

# 2. Objective

Implement the complete reasoning engine responsible for transforming AI perception into an operational decision.

At completion, the Decision Engine shall be capable of:

* Accepting structured perception results.
* Creating and maintaining reasoning state.
* Determining whether evidence is required.
* Planning evidence acquisition.
* Processing returned observations.
* Updating reasoning state.
* Determining when reasoning should stop.
* Producing a structured decision object.

The Decision Engine SHALL NOT execute tools, calculate confidence, or communicate directly with external services.

# 6. Decision Engine Responsibilities

The Decision Engine owns only reasoning.

Its responsibilities are divided into seven logical stages.

---

## Stage 1 — Receive Perception

Input:

Structured perception generated by the Vision Module.

Responsibilities:

* Validate perception.
* Normalize inputs.
* Create initial reasoning context.

No conclusions are made during this stage.

---

## Stage 2 — Initial Reasoning

Using perception,

the engine determines:

* what is already known,
* what remains uncertain,
* whether evidence is required.

No tool execution occurs.

Only reasoning.

---

## Stage 3 — Evidence Planning

If additional evidence is required,

the engine determines:

* which evidence is required,
* why it is required,
* which tool can provide it.

The output is an abstract Tool Request.

---

## Stage 4 — Observation Processing

When observations return,

the engine:

* validates observations,
* updates reasoning state,
* resolves uncertainties,
* records evidence usage.

---

## Stage 5 — Re-evaluation

After every observation,

the engine evaluates:

* Is the decision complete?
* Is another observation required?
* Have runtime limits been reached?

---

## Stage 6 — Stop Evaluation

The engine determines whether reasoning should terminate.

Reasons include:

* sufficient evidence,
* maximum iterations,
* no useful tools remain,
* contradictory evidence,
* runtime constraints.

---

## Stage 7 — Decision Generation

Generate a structured operational recommendation.

The recommendation SHALL NOT contain confidence values.

Confidence is evaluated later.

---

# 7. Decision State

The Decision State represents the complete reasoning context throughout execution.

It acts as the single source of truth for reasoning.

Every reasoning update SHALL modify only this state.

No hidden runtime state is permitted.

---

## Responsibilities

Maintain:

* perception
* evidence collected
* observations
* unresolved questions
* tool requests
* reasoning history
* iteration count
* stopping status
* final recommendation

---

## Characteristics

The Decision State SHALL be:

* deterministic
* serializable
* immutable by external components
* internally versioned during execution

Only the Decision Engine may modify the Decision State.

# Part B – Reasoning & Evidence Planning

---

# 11. Reasoning Lifecycle

The Decision Engine executes a deterministic reasoning lifecycle.

It SHALL never perform unrestricted autonomous reasoning.

Every reasoning cycle follows the same execution pattern.

```text
Receive Perception
        │
        ▼
Analyze Current Knowledge
        │
        ▼
Identify Uncertainty
        │
        ▼
Need Evidence?
     │         │
    No        Yes
     │         │
     ▼         ▼
Generate     Create Tool
Decision     Request
     │         │
     │         ▼
     │    Wait for
     │   Observation
     │         │
     └─────────┘
          │
          ▼
Update Decision State
          │
          ▼
Stopping Evaluation
```

The reasoning lifecycle SHALL remain deterministic regardless of runtime environment.

---

# 12. Initial Reasoning

Initial reasoning begins immediately after a valid perception result is received.

Its objective is **not** to produce a final recommendation.

Its objective is to determine:

* what is already known,
* what remains uncertain,
* whether external evidence is required.

---

## Responsibilities

The Decision Engine SHALL:

* validate perception output,
* identify confirmed facts,
* identify assumptions,
* identify missing information,
* initialize the reasoning context,
* initialize the iteration counter.

---

## Outputs

Initial reasoning produces:

* Updated Decision State
* Known facts
* Outstanding questions
* Evidence requirements

No Tool Requests are executed during this stage.

---

# 13. Evidence Planning

Evidence Planning determines whether additional information is required before an operational recommendation can be made.

The Decision Engine SHALL request evidence only when it materially improves decision quality.

---

## Decision Criteria

Evidence may be requested when:

* issue classification remains ambiguous,
* duplicate likelihood is uncertain,
* severity cannot be confidently determined,
* department assignment requires context,
* geographic context is required,
* environmental information is incomplete.

---

## Evidence Planning Rules

The planner SHALL:

* request only one tool at a time,
* avoid duplicate evidence requests,
* prioritize the highest-value uncertainty,
* terminate if no useful evidence exists.

Evidence requests SHALL always include justification.

---

# 14. Tool Request Generation

The Decision Engine never executes tools.

Instead, it generates abstract Tool Requests.

---

## Tool Request Responsibilities

Every Tool Request SHALL specify:

* required tool,
* execution reason,
* required inputs,
* expected observation,
* originating reasoning step.

The Runtime Coordinator will later execute these requests.

---

## Selection Principles

Tool selection SHALL be:

* deterministic,
* explainable,
* bounded,
* reproducible.

The engine SHALL never select multiple tools simultaneously.

---

# 15. Observation Processing

Observations returned by the Runtime Coordinator become inputs to the Decision Engine.

The engine SHALL validate every observation before incorporating it into the reasoning state.

---

## Responsibilities

Observation Processing SHALL:

* validate observation structure,
* reject malformed observations,
* associate observations with originating requests,
* update confirmed facts,
* remove resolved uncertainties,
* append reasoning history.

---

## Observation Integration

Every accepted observation SHALL modify only the Decision State.

No other component may maintain independent reasoning state.

---

# 16. Iteration Management

The Decision Engine performs reasoning through bounded iterations.

Each iteration consists of:

```text
Current State

↓

Reason

↓

Need Evidence?

↓

Observation

↓

Update State

↓

Repeat
```

---

## Iteration Counter

The engine SHALL maintain an explicit iteration counter.

The counter SHALL increase after every completed reasoning cycle.

The counter SHALL become part of the Decision State.

---

## Maximum Iterations

The Decision Engine SHALL never exceed the architectural runtime limits defined in Chapter 5.

When limits are reached:

* reasoning terminates,
* confidence evaluation proceeds,
* Human Review may later be recommended.

---

# 17. Stopping Controller

The Stopping Controller determines when reasoning should terminate.

It prevents:

* infinite loops,
* redundant evidence gathering,
* unnecessary latency,
* unpredictable behaviour.

---

## Valid Stop Conditions

Reasoning SHALL terminate when one or more of the following conditions become true.

### Condition 1

Sufficient evidence exists.

---

### Condition 2

No unresolved uncertainty remains.

---

### Condition 3

Maximum tool invocation limit reached.

---

### Condition 4

No additional useful evidence can be obtained.

---

### Condition 5

Further reasoning would not materially improve the recommendation.

---

### Condition 6

Runtime safety limits require termination.

---

## Invalid Behaviour

The Decision Engine SHALL NEVER:

* continue indefinitely,
* repeatedly request identical evidence,
* ignore stopping conditions,
* override runtime limits.

---

# 18. Decision Generation

Once reasoning terminates,

the Decision Engine generates a structured operational decision.

This represents the complete reasoning output before confidence evaluation.

---

## Decision Responsibilities

Generate:

* issue classification,
* recommended department,
* operational priority,
* supporting evidence,
* reasoning explanation,
* unresolved uncertainties,
* escalation recommendation (without confidence).

---

## Decision Characteristics

The decision SHALL be:

* deterministic,
* structured,
* serializable,
* explainable.

Natural-language reasoning may be included only where defined by the approved AI contracts.

---

# 19. Reasoning History

The Decision Engine SHALL maintain a complete reasoning history.

The reasoning history exists to support:

* observability,
* debugging,
* future timeline visualization,
* Human Review,
* auditability.

---

## Contents

Reasoning history SHALL record:

* reasoning step,
* evidence requested,
* observation received,
* reasoning update,
* stop decision.

The history SHALL never expose internal prompts.
